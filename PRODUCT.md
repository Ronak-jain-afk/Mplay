# Product

## Register

product

## Users
Desktop music enthusiasts who manage local audio libraries and value a clean, fast, and high-fidelity listening experience. They use the app in the background while working or in the foreground to explore lyrics and curate playlists, requiring a distraction-free and aesthetically pleasing interface.

## Product Purpose
A local-first desktop audio player that brings modern streaming-app aesthetics and features (like timed lyrics syncing, smart queues, and cover-art caching) to local libraries. Success is a lightweight, instantly responsive app that feels as polished as YouTube Music without the overhead of web-streaming.

## Brand Personality
Sleek, immersive, and premium.
3-Word Personality: Immersive, Responsive, Tactile.
Emotional Goals: Calm focus, satisfying feedback, visual delight.

## Anti-references
- Cluttered 2000s-era media players (too many small buttons, thick borders, visual clutter).
- Generic card grids that make every item look identical.
- Low-contrast, washed-out gray text that makes the UI hard to read in dark environments.
- AI-slop grammar (unnecessary numbered prefixes, small all-caps eyebrows above every section).

## Design Principles
1. **Content First, Chrome Second**: The album art, track titles, and lyrics are the focus. Keep borders, panels, and chrome minimal or transparent.
2. **Tactile Interaction**: Sliders, buttons, and state changes must provide clear, smooth, and physically satisfying feedback (e.g. hover states, click transitions).
3. **Continuous Flow**: Never interrupt the listener. Track changes, view switches, and panels slide or fade smoothly to maintain the music-listening flow.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
- Contrast ratios of at least 4.5:1 for all text (even on dark backgrounds, using bright, clear foreground tints rather than muddy grays).
- Full keyboard-navigation support for playback controls.
- Reduced motion support to immediately bypass animations if requested by the OS.
